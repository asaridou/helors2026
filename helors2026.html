About the Conference

HELORS 2026 — Advancing Operational Research

The Hellenic Operational Research Society (HELORS) was founded in 1963 and has since been the principal scientific body promoting Operations Research and Management Science in Greece. The annual national conference is the flagship event bringing together researchers, academicians, and practitioners from Greece and abroad.

For 2026, the conference is hosted by the Department of Production & Management Engineering of the Democritus University of Thrace (DUTh) in Xanthi. This is a unique opportunity to engage with cutting-edge research in OR/MS in the vibrant academic city of Thrace.

The conference welcomes conceptual, methodological, and empirical contributions in all fields of Operational Research / Management Science (OR/MS), Artificial Intelligence, Decision Support Systems, and related disciplines. We invite researchers, academicians, practitioners, and students to participate and present their current work.

Conference Scope

Purpose & Objectives

The purpose of the HELORS 2026 Conference is to disseminate recent scientific advances stemming from the interactions of Operational Research / Management Science (OR/MS) with Artificial Intelligence, Data Science, and modern applications in industry and society, as well as to promote international co-operation among researchers and practitioners working in these fields.

The conference aims to serve as a vibrant forum where theory meets practice: participants can present and discuss novel algorithms, models, and frameworks, as well as real-world case studies demonstrating the impact of OR/MS in domains ranging from supply chain management and healthcare to energy systems and smart cities.

We invite all researchers, academicians, practitioners, and students to participate and present their current research in Operational Research by means of methodological tools or techniques. All submitted abstracts will be peer-reviewed by the scientific committee for relevance and quality.

Please note that the submission must be done by the person who will present the paper and will be listed as the first author. Each submitted paper needs to be covered by one unique registration — two submitted papers require two separate registrations.

Topics of Interest

Conference Themes

HELORS 2026 welcomes contributions across all areas of OR/MS. The following thematic tracks are representative but the call is open to all relevant research.

🔢

Mathematical Programming & Optimization

Linear, integer, nonlinear, stochastic, and robust optimization; exact and heuristic solution methods.

🤖

Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

AI-driven decision support, ML for OR, neural networks, deep learning in management science.

🏭

Supply Chain & Logistics

Inventory management, vehicle routing, facility location, distribution network design.

📊

Multicriteria Decision Analysis

MCDM/MCDA methods, preference modeling, outranking approaches, aggregation operators.

🏥

Healthcare & Social Systems

Hospital resource allocation, health policy evaluation, OR in public and social sector applications.

⚡

Energy & Sustainability

Renewable energy optimization, smart grids, energy policy models, climate-related decisions.

🌐

Networks & Graph Theory

Network flows, scheduling, combinatorial optimization, transportation network analysis.

📈

Data Science & Analytics

Prescriptive analytics, simulation, forecasting, stochastic modeling, big data applications in OR.

🏗️

Project & Operations Management

Project scheduling, quality management, production planning, lean and agile methodologies.

💹

Financial Modeling & Risk Analysis

Portfolio optimization, credit risk, financial decision support, OR in banking and insurance.

🎮

Game Theory & Mechanism Design

Cooperative and non-cooperative games, auctions, bargaining, competitive strategy modeling.

🏙️

Smart Cities & Regional Development

Urban planning models, geospatial optimization, regional policy, infrastructure design.
